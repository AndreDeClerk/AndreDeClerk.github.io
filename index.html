Cover Letter

Technology has advanced far more than we could imagine. With web3 and blockchain technology coming into play companies need employees that ready for change. An employee that is willing to do the research and development to learn new skills to be ahead of the curve. This will ultimately make workflow more efficient and effective. While there are many tools out there like SQL, Tableau and Python, more will emerge in the coming years and companies will need an employee that embraces change.

I graduated from George Mason University, with a degree in Information Systems and Operations Management. I have a strong work ethic, customer service skills, a great enthusiastic attitude, and work effectively alone or in a team environment. I held leadership roles throughout my college career when working on team projects. I made sure to ask each team member what part of the project they would prefer to focus on, I would do the portion that no team member would prefer to do. This leads me doing more challenging and technical work, while also aiding other team members on their part if needed.

During COVID, I adapted and thrived with distance learning. While some classmates struggled with understanding material since professors were difficult to reach, I helped groups of students on zoom understand the material. I found a passion in teaching people about various subject matter, this followed me with my social media business where I taught people how to reach a wider audience.

As a sophomore at George Mason, I worked as a Food Runner for Sweetwater Tavern. Soon after, I worked as a Sales Associate at SuperDry. These jobs improved many transferable skills like: problem solving, communication, and customer service. Then I got a Business Analyst internship at Praescient Analytics, the job required me to analyze prospective contracts worthy of bidding on. After we obtained the contract, I would also help with recruiting technical positions for the contracts that Praescient won. The intern team would also work with various projects using specialty software like Analyst Notebook, Syntelix and Data Minr. One project was data points of the movement of Chinese battleships in the south China sea and with that software we had to interpret and predict where they would go in the future. This was an interesting project because 80% of our predictions came true.

When COVID made Praescient telework I learned how to communicate remotely in my internship as well as in school. Unfortunately, SuperDry closed therefore I had to be innovative to make money. I started my Social Media Business where I eventually was able to manage hundreds of accounts, while maintaining my full-time status at George Mason University. I use my budgeting, advertising, networking, and promotion skills to expand that business taking advantage that everyone needed and wanted to market online. I used past clients as references and would offer some free trial to help them trust me and my work. I established networks in many cities like Los Angeles, Chicago, New York, and Miami.

Once graduated I understood that companies want to see practical skills being used so I started to work on a portfolio project, which is what this website is. You can see how I retrieved and visualized the different data sets. Lastly, I have started to study for my 1Z0-082: Oracle Database Administration exam and I am interested in getting a Oracle Certified Professional (OCP) in the next couple of years.
